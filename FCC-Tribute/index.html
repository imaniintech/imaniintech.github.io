The Life of Frida Kahlo

  • 1907-Born in Coyoacan, Mexico City.
  • 1913-Contracts polio, which leaves Frida with a slightly crooked right foot.
  • 1922-Attends the Escuela Nacional Preparatoria with intent to study medicine. She takes admiration for artist Diego Rivera artwork.
  • 1925-Frida and her friend Alejandro Arias were involved in a collision between a tram and bus. Frida was seriously injured and spent a month in the Red Cross Hospital where she would paint.
  • 1928-Frida joins the communist party and is reaquainted with Diego Rivera. They fall in love.
  • 1928-Frida and Diego marry. Frida leaves the Communist Party due to Diego's expulsion.
  • 1930-Frida experiences her first miscarraige.
  • 1931-As pain increased in Frida's right leg, so does her fame and recognition in artistry. She meets Dr.Leo Eloesser who becomse her most trusted medical advisor.
  • 1932-Kahlo experience her second miscarraige. A few months later, her mother dies during a gall-bladder operation.
  • 1934-Due to "infantillism of the ovaries", Frida's third pregnacy is terminated at three months. She also has a foot operation where several toes were removed.
    Frida's husband Diego also has an affair with her younger sister, Cristina.
  • 1935-Frida seperates from Diego.
  • 1936-Experience third operation on her foot and becomes actively engaged in a solidarity committe in aid of the Spanish Republicans.
  • 1938-First solo exhibition in Julien Levy's gallery in New York.
  • 1939-Frida continues her travels to Paris where she exhibit more of her work. By the end of the year, Frida and Diego are divorced.
  • 1940-Remarried Diego. The next year, her father Guillermo Kahlo dies of a heart attack.
  • 1942-Frida started writing a diary and becomes a member of the Seminario de Cultura Mexicana. In 1943, Frida becomes a professor at La Esmeralda School but due to poor health, continues classes from her home.
  • 1944-Has an operation on her spine and four years later, rejoins the Mexican Communist Party.
  • 1949-Becomes hospitalized and undergoes a total of seven opartions on her spine and spend nine months in the hospital.
  • 1951-Although confined to a wheelchair, Frida supports the Mexico Peace movement and helps collect signaturees.
  • 1954-Frida passes away
